# Industry 4.0

The fourth industrial revolution -- smart manufacturing using cyber-physical systems, IoT, cloud computing, and AI to create interconnected, data-driven production environments.

## Key Technologies

- Cyber-physical systems (CPS)
- Internet of Things (IoT) and industrial IoT
- Cloud and edge computing
- AI and machine learning in manufacturing
- Digital twins
- Additive manufacturing (3D printing)

## Connection to Previous Revolutions

1. **Industry 1.0**: Mechanization (steam power)
2. **Industry 2.0**: Mass production and [[scientific-management]]
3. **Industry 3.0**: Automation (PLCs, CNC)
4. **Industry 4.0**: Cyber-physical integration and AI

## Relationship to [[mass-customization]]

Industry 4.0 enables mass customization at unprecedented scale. Smart factories can produce personalized products on the same line as standardized ones, switching configurations automatically based on digital specs.
