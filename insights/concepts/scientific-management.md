# Scientific Management (Taylorism)

Frederick Winslow Taylor's system (1911) of analyzing and synthesizing workflows for economic efficiency. Studied tasks scientifically to find the "one best way" to perform each task.

## Key Principles

- Scientific study of tasks to determine optimal methods
- Matching workers to jobs based on capability
- Training workers in optimal methods
- Detailed planning and monitoring by management

## Historical Role

Led to [[mass-production]] and the assembly line. [[henry-ford]] adapted Taylor's scientific management with the moving assembly line in 1913.

## Criticism

- Dehumanizing: treats workers as cogs
- Over-specialization: repetitive stress injuries, monotony
- Superseded by [[lean-production]] which adds worker agency and continuous improvement
